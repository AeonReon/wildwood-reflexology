Meet Lauren

Why I Became a Reflexologist

For 16 years I worked in food manufacturing — food safety, quality, the works. It was a good career, but it wasn't my calling.

That changed during my own 7-year fertility journey. Reflexology became a lifeline for me during that incredibly difficult time. It was the one thing that helped me feel like myself again — calmer, more balanced, more hopeful.

That experience changed my life. I retrained, earned my Level 5 Diploma in Practitioner Reflexology (the highest regulated qualification in the UK and Ireland), and built Wildwood Reflexology to help others the way reflexology helped me.

I've since completed specialist training in fertility, pregnancy, postnatal, palliative, menopause, and NEPIP (stress and anxiety) reflexology — most recently training specifically in menopause support last year. I also offer cranial acupressure and facial rejuvenation acupressure. Every person who walks through my door gets a treatment tailored specifically to them.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you might want to know before your first reflexology session, plus the important professional information.

Everyone is different and the response to reflexology can be immediate or sometimes take longer than one appointment, but often benefits can be seen after 4–5 sessions. Regular weekly treatments are best for the body to properly begin its self-healing process. Following an initial consultation I'll suggest the number of reflexology sessions I think we should undertake together.

Reflexology is performed entirely on the feet, ankles or hands — so the most you'll need to do is take off your socks and roll up your trousers. During the treatment you'll lie back on a reclining chair, supported by pillows and covered with a soft blanket to keep you warm.

And don't worry — reflexology doesn't tickle. Many clients come to me with ticklish feet or worried about their feet being touched, but find they can easily relax and enjoy the benefits.

You might find some areas feel sensitive to the touch, but reflexology does not involve hard pressing in the way something like a sports massage might. As a therapist I'll be aware of the status of your feet — noticing how they feel, the temperature and colour. All these things play a part in understanding which areas of your body, as reflected through your feet, require a bit more care and attention.

No — reflexologists cannot claim to diagnose, cure or prescribe. However, during a treatment we get 'feedback' as to which parts of the body are working well and which are not. Sometimes reflexology will highlight a specific area which is struggling or working hard to maintain your wellbeing; if a particular reflex point shows this kind of reaction I'll discuss it with you at the end of the session.

Reflexology is a complementary therapy and should not be considered an alternative to conventional medicine.

Reflexology is a safe, non-invasive complementary therapy suitable for all ages, from baby to adult. It's usually safe to have reflexology alongside conventional medicine, but if you are currently having any medical treatment please speak to your GP before booking an appointment.

There are however a small number of total contraindications — conditions where I'm unable to treat:

  • Thrombosis / DVT
  • Unstable heart condition
  • Imminent medical test
  • Contagious or notifiable disease
  • Undergoing active radiotherapy

If you're unsure, please get in touch and I'll happily talk through it with you.

Other clients get back in touch when they start feeling aches and pains, arranging treatments as and when they need them. There is never any pressure to book more sessions than you want.

Reflexology is a wonderful way of maintaining your body's healthy balance and general wellbeing. Clients commonly come to me for support with stress and tension, sleep issues, headaches and migraines, women's health concerns, fertility and pregnancy support, hormonal imbalances, IBS, sinusitis, hay fever, and general feelings of being run-down.

While it is not a cure, reflexology can be particularly helpful in managing many ongoing or recurring conditions that we might have come to accept over time as being a normal part of life.
